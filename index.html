Tribute to Robert Cailliau


Who is Robert Cailliau?

Robert Cailliau Is a Belgian computer scientist and author who suggested the first hypertext system for CERN in 1987 and helped develop the World Wide Web. He was born in Tongeren, Belgium on the 26th of January, 1947. He graduated from Ghent University in 1969 with a degree in civil engineering and mechanical engineering. He also got a Masters degree in Computer, Information and Control engineering.

Accomplishments

  • 1990: Robert Cailliau co-authors a proposal for funding the creation of the World Wide Web with Tim Berners-Lee.
  • 1992: Robert Cailliau develops MacWWW, the first web browser for the classic Mac OS operating system
  • 1992: Robert Cailliau starts the European Commission's first web-based project for information dissemination in Europe.
  • 1993: Robert Cailliau calls for the first International WWW Conference. It was then held at CERN in May of 1994.
  • 1995: Robert Cailliau starts the “Web for Schools” project to bring the web into classrooms as a resource for education.
